Yichi Zhang

PhD Candidate, Department of Computer Science
Tsinghua University

Email: zyc22 [AT] tsinghua [DOT] edu [DOT] cn | tibo_ricky [AT] outlook [DOT] com

I am a fourth-year Ph.D. candidate (expected to graduate in June, 2027) in the Department of Computer Science and Technology, Tsinghua University, advised by Prof. Jun Zhu. Before that, I received my B.S. degree from the Department of Computer Science and Technology, Tsinghua University, and my secondary B.S. degree from the Department of Psychology, Tsinghua University, in June, 2022. I work closely with Prof. Hang Su and Prof. Yinpeng Dong. I am also honored to have visited TML Lab led by Prof. Tongliang Liu. I am now a visiting PhD student in van de Schaar Lab at the University of Cambridge.

My research focuses on large language models and multimodal models, with an emphasis on post-training alignment and evaluation for real-world deployment. I study how to improve the reliability and stability of model behaviors, therefore reducing undesired outputs including harmful content, hallucination, overthinking, etc. I also work on evaluation frameworks and stress-testing methodologies for LLMs and MLLMs to systematically uncover failure modes before deployment. I look forward to collaborating with leading research groups in the related fields.

I am open to opportunities for collaboration and academic exchange in any form. Please feel free to contact me if you are interested.
